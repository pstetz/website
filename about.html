Some miscellaneous classes I took were: speed rubik's cubing, meditation, Wes Anderson movies, and playing the carillon.

After college, I was lucky enough to land an internship at a smart lighting startup called Noon Home. For 6 months, I helped in the formation of the bulb detection algorithm and wrote scripts for mass testing of the firmware. I spent a lot of time testing the wide variety of bulbs the universe has to offer along with any edge case that might arise. My new party trick is identifying the type, wattage, and brand of any bulb I see.

From December to presently, I've been working at another startup called Last Minute Gear. It's essentially full stack developer work that's shared between me and the founder. I think the business model is very clever and it's a more environmentally friendly alternative than the consumerism of Amazon. Unfortunately, my savings account and paranoia to save money are placing a deadline on how long I can work there. I've talked with the founder about transitioning into a different company.

I love solving puzzles and keeping my mind active. I hope to make the world better in any way I can.

Let's be friends!
